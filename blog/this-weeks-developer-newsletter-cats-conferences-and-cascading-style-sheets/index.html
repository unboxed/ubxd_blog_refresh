Ruby Manor 4: CFP closes Monday 4th, Voting starts shortly after.

http://blog.rubymanor.org/post/44288384490/get-your-proposals-in-by-monday-4th-march

The ruby conference I’m co-organising might be sold out, but that doesn’t mean you can’t get in! All our speakers will be given a ticket if they don’t have one. The CFP closes on Monday 4th, so there’s still time to propose a talk: http://vestibule.rubymanor.org/proposals/new. The CFP is completely open and anonymous, so even if you’re not going to propose something, you should sign up and leave some comments. In a few days time we’ll open up voting and let the community pick the talks they want to see on the day. If you don’t have a ticket, you can still get involved and vote as we film all the talks and release them to the world, so your opinions are still valid.

## Um, what? Corner (aka Cat Corner)

http://bringinthecats.com/ - Steve B

Um, What?

https://twitter.com/CatUserStories - Matt P

http://www.filldisk.com/ - Matt P

Read the name of this site carefully. Visit it, sure, but do not leave it open in a tab unless you’re using Firefox. OK, now that the warnings are out of the way I shall explain a little bit more. It’s a site which gives an endless stream of cats (who doesn’t love such sites?) using the HTML 5 localStorage. There are problems with the implementations of many popular browsers meaning that this storage can be abused. More details explained on the site. Dare you click?
